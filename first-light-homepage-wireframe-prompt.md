# Addendum Prompt: Homepage Wireframe (from Niamh's sketch)

Paste this alongside `first-light-marketing-website-prompt.md`. It's a literal transcription of Niamh's hand-drawn homepage wireframe (`niamh_plan.jpg`) — build the homepage to match this layout and copy exactly; don't invent a different structure.

## Navigation

Simple top nav, three links only: **About | Services | Contact**. No separate "Home" nav item — the logo (top-left, linked to `/`) serves as the home link.

## Homepage layout, top to bottom

**1. Hero band — coral background (`#FF5757`)**
- Centered sunburst/sunrise motif (the same radiating-lines graphic from the logo) above the wordmark.
- "First Light" wordmark centered beneath it, in the serif display font.
- Treat this as a compact full-width hero band (not a huge, sparse hero) — it's essentially the logo lockup rendered large, matching `assets/logos/1.png`.

**2. Intro section — cream background (`#FFE9DD`), two-column layout**
- **Left column:** headline copy — confirmed final wording is **"Bringing clarity to your marketing."** (Her sketch shows this line was drafted/revised a few times before landing here — treat it as final, don't paraphrase it.) Give it room to breathe; this is the primary value proposition statement on the page. A short supporting sentence or CTA button (e.g. "Get in touch" → `/contact`) can sit beneath it even though it's not explicitly sketched — use judgment for a complete hero, but don't override the headline wording.
- **Right column:** an image. Use a photo of Niamh if available (the proposal deck includes one — her standing by a lake, from `assets/proposal/1.png`) or a placeholder if you'd rather source a proper headshot later. Sketch just labels this "image," so any warm, on-brand photography placeholder is acceptable for v1.

**3. Capability strip — directly below the two-column section**
- A single thin horizontal bar/strip of text: **"Strategy • Content • Campaigns • Marketing Support"**
- This reads as a compact one-line summary of what she does, not a full services section with cards/descriptions — keep it minimal (small caps or a subtle divider-style treatment, similar to a "trusted by" strip but text-only).

Flag this back to me/Niamh and confirm the definitive services list before building out the `/services` page — don't guess.
